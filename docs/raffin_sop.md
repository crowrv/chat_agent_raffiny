# Raffin Cake — Standard Operating Procedures (SOP)

**Business:** Raffin Cake — Korean/Asian-style fresh cream cakes, roll cakes, and desserts
**Operator:** Single baker (owner-operator)
**License:** California MEHKO (Microenterprise Home Kitchen Operation), Permit #PT0502833 — a separate program from Cottage Food, requiring a health permit, in-person kitchen inspection, and Food Protection Manager Certification
**Model:** Online order intake → made-to-order → pickup (primary) or delivery (by request)
**Contact:** business@raffin.studio · www.raffin.studio · IG @raffin_cake

---

| Activity | Window |

## 2. Lead Times

| Order type | Minimum lead time | Recommended |
|------------|-------------------|-------------|
| Standard menu cakes & rolls | 48 hours before pickup | 3–4 days |
| Custom cakes (custom design, color, topper, message) | 1 week before pickup | 10–14 days |
| Large orders (half-sheet, multiple cakes, events) | 2 weeks before pickup | 3 weeks |
| Wedding / tiered cakes | 4 weeks before pickup | 6+ weeks |

**Rush policy:** If a customer requests inside the lead-time window, warn them plainly, suggest a later pickup, and only accept if they explicitly confirm. Flag the order as **Rushed** in the order sheet so the baker can decide whether capacity allows.

**Daily capacity cap:** Maximum 3 custom cakes OR 1 custom + 4 standard cakes per production day. Do not overbook — the baker works alone.

## 3. Order Intake Workflow

1. **Receive inquiry** via chat agent, Google Form, Instagram DM, or email.
2. **Discover the occasion** (birthday, anniversary, baby shower, etc.) and required date.
3. **Verify availability** against the Raffin Data Google Sheet (Pickup Locations tab for blocked dates; production calendar for daily cap).
4. **Build the order** one question at a time: cake type → size → sponge → cream → toppings → design → message → allergens → reference images.
5. **Quote price** from the live Product List tab. Never guess pricing.
6. **Collect customer info:** full name, phone, email, pickup location, agreed pickup time.
7. **Present order summary** and wait for explicit confirmation.
8. **Create Google Calendar pickup event** with customer invite (skip if delivery or out-of-window — see Section 7).
9. **Save order row** in the Orders tab of the Google Sheet.
10. **Send confirmation email** from business@raffin.studio (BCC the business inbox).
11. **Send payment instructions** (see Section 5).

## 6. Production Workflow (per order)

**Day before pickup (or same morning for next-day pickups):**

1. **Pre-shift sanitation** — wipe down all surfaces, sanitize tools, wash hands, put on clean apron and hair cover.
2. **Mise en place** — pull ingredients from refrigerator, verify lot/expiration dates, weigh out per recipe card.
3. **Bake sponge** — vanilla / matcha / chocolate / seasonal as ordered. Cool fully on rack before moving.
4. **Prep fruit & fillings** — wash, dry, slice fruit immediately before assembly. Whip cream just before use (do not pre-whip the night before).
5. **Assemble & frost** — layer sponge, cream, fruit; crumb-coat; final coat; chill 15 min between coats.
6. **Decorate** — piping, fruit arrangement, name plaque / message. Read the order sheet twice to verify spelling.
7. **Photograph** — top-down + side photo of finished cake for portfolio / IG.
8. **Box & label** — see Section 8.
9. **Refrigerate** at 38–40°F until pickup.
10. **Post-shift sanitation** — wash, sanitize, dry all tools; mop floor; take out trash; log production in baker's notebook.

## 7. Delivery (By Request Only)

- **Do not proactively offer delivery.** Default is pickup.
- If asked: "We can offer delivery for an additional fee if the schedule works on our end. Share the address and preferred time and we'll follow up by email."
- Collect: delivery address, desired date/time window.
- **Do not quote a fee in chat.** Baker reviews and emails a fee quote within 24 hours.
- Mark the order as **Pending — delivery requested, needs team review**.
- Skip the pickup calendar invite; deliveries are confirmed by email after the baker reviews route + fee.

**Delivery fee guidance (internal):**
- $3/mile with minimum of $25
- 50+ mi: case-by-case

## 8. Cancellation & Refund Policy

| Timing | Policy |
|--------|--------|
| 7+ days before pickup | Deposit transferable to a future date within 60 days; no cash refund |
| 3–6 days before pickup | Deposit forfeit; remaining balance not charged |
| Less than 72 hours before pickup | Full order amount due (ingredients purchased, production scheduled) |
| Raffin-initiated cancellation (illness, emergency) | Full refund OR free reschedule, customer's choice |

- Refunds are processed by the baker, not the chat agent. Escalate cancellation requests to business@raffin.studio.
- Document every cancellation in the Orders sheet: change `Order Status` to `Cancelled` and note the reason and refund decision.

## 9. Customer Communication Standards

- **Response time:** within 24 hours for chat / form / DM; same-day for active orders.
- **Tone:** warm, personal, craft-focused. Cakes are described in sensory terms (airy, light, fresh).
- **Language:** match the customer's language (Korean ↔ English). Never mix in a single message.
- **Read-back rule:** always read back the exact cake message text and pickup date/time before confirming.
- **Photo updates:** for custom cakes, send a photo of the finished cake before pickup so the customer knows it's ready.
- **No competitor talk.** Stay focused on Raffin Cake.

## 12. Record Keeping

The Raffin Data Google Sheet (`주문` tab) is the source of truth. It is a **formula-driven ledger** — the agent writes only the input cells of the first empty row and the formulas compute the rest. Per-order the agent fills:

`order date · pickup date · pickup time · Customer name · Cake type · Size · Qty · Lettering? (TRUE/FALSE in col V) · Lettering message (col AM "Memo")`

- **Order ID auto-generates** in column D via formula (`YYMM###`, e.g. `2606003`) — do not hand-generate it.
- **Total auto-computes** in column W from the Product List. **Column AB "Received" stays EMPTY until the deposit is paid and the booking is confirmed** — it records money actually in hand, which the baker fills on payment.
- The sheet has **no columns** for phone, email, address, allergens, design, or reference images. Those live in the **confirmation email** (and the baker's separate customer registry), never the order row.

**Retention:** keep all order rows indefinitely for tax and trend analysis. Customer PII (phone, email, address) is never shared outside the business.

**Production notebook:** the baker keeps a physical notebook with daily production notes (yield, issues, ingredient lots) — minimum 1 year retention per MEHKO best practice.

## 10. Escalation — When to Loop in the Baker

The chat agent should escalate to the baker (business@raffin.studio) when:

- Customer requests out-of-window pickup time
- Customer requests delivery (always — for fee/schedule confirmation)
- Custom design that requires the baker's judgment on feasibility
- Order over $300 or for 50+ servings
- Cancellation, refund, or modification of an existing order
- Allergy disclosure that may make the order infeasible
- Any complaint, quality issue, or pickup no-show
- Any question the agent cannot answer from the SOP / FAQ / sheet

Escalation message to customer:
> "Let me check with the Raffin team to make sure we get this right — we'll follow up by email shortly."
